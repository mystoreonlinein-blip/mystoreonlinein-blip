Onboarding

The onboarding starts after the store has paid for the subscription and has provided all the information that is needed to start processing online orders. We will need details like:

  • GST Number
  • Address of the store
  • Phone number (for the users to allow contacting over a call in case of trouble)

Store QR Code

The store is given a unique QR code that customers can scan to visit your store directly.

Log in

The user can log in using either Google or Apple account. The app itself does not store any password for store owners.

If a given user is associated with more then one stores, the step after log in will prompt to select the store.

Because this is a cloud hosted solution, the users are free to log in from any device they chose.

Delivery enabled?

The store can chose to turn this option on or off.

If this option is turned off, the users can not have the goods dedlivered.

In store pickup enabled?

The store can chose to turn this option on or off.

If this option is turned off, the users can not have the in store pickup.

Store Status & Business Hours

Store Online/Offline Toggle

The store can use the "Store Online" toggle to immediately take the store offline regardless of business hours configuration. When disabled, customers cannot place new orders. The store owner can configure a custom offline message that will be displayed to customers when they try to open the store.

Business Hours Configuration

Configure operating hours for each day of the week independently. For each day, you can choose from three options:

  • Closed: The store does not accept orders on this day. Customers will see "Not accepting orders" for that day.
  • Set Hours: Configure specific opening and closing times (e.g., 09:00 AM to 09:00 PM). Orders are only accepted during these hours, and the store automatically goes offline outside these times.
  • 24 Hours: The store accepts orders all day on this day. Status shows "Open 24 hours".

The system automatically manages store availability based on the configured business hours, turning order acceptance on and off at the specified times each day.

Maximum Pending Orders

Control how many pending orders your store can handle at once to prevent being overwhelmed with orders you cannot fulfill.

How it works:

  • Set to 0: Unlimited orders allowed. The store will accept orders without any cap.
  • Set to a number (e.g., 100): When the number of pending orders reaches this limit, the store will automatically pause and stop accepting new orders. Customers will see a message indicating the store is temporarily overloaded.

Once pending orders are completed and the count drops below the limit, the store automatically resumes accepting orders.

Configure map regions that allow delivery

This configuration controls which areas of the city are servable by the store. Any areas outside that boundary are un-servable for the store.

Our sales team will agree with you on the region and will pass it on to the implementation team.

You will have to reach out to support for changing this option.

Are multiple orders are delivered in a single trip?

If online deliveries are enabled, the store could decide if one dispatch will happen for each order or there will be one larger trip created that will cover all the customers.

Inventory Management

Control stock tracking for your products. Some stores may choose not to track inventory, while others need precise stock management to prevent overselling.

Enable Inventory Tracking

This global setting controls whether your store tracks product inventory. When you toggle this setting, it affects your entire catalog and order processing workflow.

When Inventory Tracking is Enabled:

  • Stock availability display: Products show stock availability on the storefront, letting customers know if items are in stock
  • Inventory fields in products: The product edit page includes inventory quantity fields for tracking stock levels
  • Order validation: The order flow automatically checks stock availability before accepting orders
  • Out-of-stock prevention: Orders cannot be placed for items that are out of stock, preventing overselling
  • Automatic stock updates: Stock levels are automatically decremented when orders are confirmed

When Inventory Tracking is Disabled:

  • No stock tracking or inventory fields in product management
  • Orders can be placed regardless of stock levels
  • Suitable for stores with unlimited stock, made-to-order items, or service-based offerings

This flexibility allows different types of businesses to use the platform - from stores with limited inventory that need precise tracking, to stores that don't need inventory constraints.

Order Workflow Automation

Configure how orders flow through your system by automating key steps in the order processing lifecycle.

Auto-Accept Orders

When enabled, incoming orders are automatically confirmed without requiring manual approval from the store owner. When disabled, each order must be manually reviewed and accepted before moving forward in the workflow.

Auto-Start Preparing

When enabled, orders automatically move to the "Preparing" stage immediately after confirmation. When disabled, the store owner must manually start preparation for each order.

Workflow Combinations:

  • Manual confirm → Manual prepare: Both toggles off - full manual control over order processing
  • Manual confirm → Auto prepare: Auto-Accept off, Auto-Start Preparing on - manually accept orders, then they automatically move to preparation
  • Auto confirm → Manual prepare: Auto-Accept on, Auto-Start Preparing off - orders are automatically accepted but require manual trigger to start preparation
  • Auto confirm → Auto prepare: Both toggles on - fully automated workflow from order placement to preparation

The system displays your current workflow configuration to help you understand how orders will be processed.

Partial Fulfillment

Handle out-of-stock situations gracefully by offering customers the option to proceed with only the available items instead of canceling the entire order.

How Partial Fulfillment Works:

  1. Store identifies unavailable items: When preparing an order, the store discovers some items are out of stock or unavailable
  2. Store marks items as unavailable: The store removes the unavailable items from the order and specifies the reason (e.g., "Insufficient inventory")
  3. Customer receives notification: The customer is notified that some items are unavailable and is shown the modified order with only available items
  4. Customer makes decision: The customer can choose to either:
    • Proceed with the partial order (only available items)
    • Cancel the entire order
  5. Order updates: If customer approves, the order proceeds with updated items and pricing. If customer cancels, the order is canceled

Store Owner Interface (Marking Unavailable Items):

When the store needs to mark items as unavailable, they use the "Send Order to Customer for Review" dialog:

  • Item selection: Checkboxes next to each order item to mark which ones cannot be fulfilled
  • Item details: Each item shows tier, quantity, price, and total for reference
  • Real-time summary: Shows count of items to mark unavailable vs. items that can be fulfilled
  • Clear instructions: "Select items that cannot be fulfilled. The customer will be notified and can decide whether to proceed with the remaining items or cancel the order."
  • Send to Customer button: Triggers notification to customer for approval decision

Customer Notification View:

After the store sends the partial fulfillment request, the customer receives a notification:

  • Alert title: "Some Items Unavailable" with "Partial Fulfillment Pending" badge
  • Clear message: Specifies which order is affected and how many items are unavailable vs. remaining
  • Two action buttons:
    • "Proceed with Available Items" - Customer accepts the partial order
    • "Cancel Order" - Customer cancels the entire order
  • Timestamp: Shows when the notification was sent

Store Portal View After Customer Approval:

Once a customer approves partial fulfillment, the store portal displays:

  • Notification banner: "Customer approved proceeding with available items. Some items were removed from this order."
  • Order Items section: Lists the items being fulfilled with current quantities and pricing
  • Removed Items section: Shows unavailable items (struck through) with the reason for removal
  • Updated pricing: Subtotal, tax, and total reflect only the items being fulfilled

Benefits:

  • Reduced cancellations: Customers can still receive what's available instead of losing the entire order
  • Better customer experience: Transparent communication about stock issues with customer choice
  • Revenue retention: Complete partial orders instead of full cancellations
  • Clear audit trail: System maintains record of what was removed and why

Payment Tracking

Control whether to track payment information for orders. This setting determines if your store operates as a full e-commerce platform or as billing-only software.

Track Payments Toggle

This global setting controls payment tracking behavior across your entire store.

When Payment Tracking is Enabled (Default):

  • Payment method selection: Customers select payment methods during checkout (cash on delivery, online payment, UPI, etc.)
  • Payment status tracking: Orders show payment status (Paid, Pending, Failed) throughout their lifecycle
  • Payment confirmation workflow: Orders can be confirmed or held based on payment status
  • Full e-commerce mode: Complete online ordering with payment processing

When Payment Tracking is Disabled:

  • No payment selection: Customers don't choose payment methods during checkout
  • No payment status: Payment status is not displayed or tracked in the system
  • Billing-only mode: System functions purely for order management and invoicing
  • Offline payment handling: Ideal for stores that handle all payments offline, outside the system
  • B2B scenarios: Perfect for wholesale or B2B operations where invoicing happens separately

Use Cases for Disabling Payment Tracking:

  • Stores that only accept payments at physical counter or upon delivery
  • B2B businesses that invoice customers separately with net payment terms
  • Businesses using external accounting systems for payment management
  • Order management for internal operations without customer payments
